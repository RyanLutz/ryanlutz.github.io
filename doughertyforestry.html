Dougherty Forestry Manufacturing

Dougherty Forestry Manufacturing is an Oklahoma based company which specializes in forestry and agricultural equipment. A well known and sought after name in forestry equipment, Dougherty Forestry Manufacturing, builds and distributes top of the line land clearing and light forestry application attachments for skid steers, track loaders, excavators, and tractors.

My services included collaborating to develop a company and brand image, designing digital and printed marketing material, and creating a website to showcase company products. Business needs warranted a variety of printed media such as: posters, flyers, banners, brochures product manuals, and digital media which included pictures touched up and extracted, video captured and edited and a variety of web design needs.

Web Presence

turbosaw.com

ui/ux, html5/css3, responsive, CMS, PHP

Dougherty Forestry Manufacturing's website, turbosaw.com, is designed to inform customers of products available for purchase, compare their products to competitive retailers, and find a local TurboSaw™ dealer. Customers can also read detailed information about products offered by Dougherty Forestry, learn about forestry and invasive tree species, learn of upcoming shows and events Dougherty Forestry will participate in, and contact support for product trouble shooting and service.

The focus during development of the website was to lead customers with as few clicks as possible to the product that would best suit their needs.

Product Pages

CSS/HTML, Javascript/jQuery, Photoshop, Blender

Product Videos

Product videos are proven to be one of the most effective tools for advertising products such as these in action. I cut and edited videos to showcase cutting times and ease of use of the equipment. These videos where then uploaded to a video steaming service.

Product 360's

The product pages feature product 360's in a modal box. The images for the product where adapted and refined from cad drawings in Blender, then rendered and optimized. Then I implemented a javascript api specifically for product 360's.
